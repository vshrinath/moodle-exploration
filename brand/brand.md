## Voice

### Attributes
- Thoughtful, not academic
- Authoritative, not preachy
- Warm, not casual
- Curious, not contrarian
- Confident, not flashy

### Tone do's
- Write like you're speaking to a peer over coffee — informed, direct, respectful
- Use questions to invite thinking, not to hedge
- Let ideas breathe — don't overstuff sentences
- Use concrete examples over abstract claims

### Tone don'ts
- Never use "disrupt", "synergy", "leverage" (as verb), "thought leader"
- No clickbait headlines — no "You won't believe..." or "X things you need to know"
- No exclamation marks in headlines
- No filler phrases: "In today's fast-paced world...", "It goes without saying..."
- Don't oversimplify complex ideas — the audience can handle nuance

## Audience

### Primary
- Business leaders, founders, CXOs in India
- Ages 30–55
- Read long-form, subscribe to newsletters, value curation over volume
- Care about: strategy, leadership, technology, economics, culture

### What they value
- Depth over speed
- Original reporting and analysis over aggregation
- Perspectives that challenge conventional thinking
- Content they can return to — not disposable

### What they ignore
- Listicles, SEO-stuffed articles, shallow takes
- Promotional content disguised as editorial
- Social media outrage cycles

## Competitor Positioning

**We are not:**
- A breaking news outlet (that's ET, Mint, Moneycontrol)
- A hot-take machine (that's social media)
- An SEO content farm (that's content marketing agencies)
- A startup cheerleader (that's YourStory, Inc42)

**We are:**
- The place where business leaders come to think, not just to be informed
- Independent — no corporate ownership dictating editorial
- Long-term — content that's worth reading a year after publication
