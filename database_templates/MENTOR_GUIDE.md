# Mentor Guide - Database Activity Review and Approval

## Overview

As a mentor, you play a critical role in reviewing and approving trainee submissions across three database activities:

1. **Case Logbook** - Review surgical cases and provide feedback
2. **Credentialing Sheet** - Verify monthly procedure counts
3. **Research Publications** - Review research proposals and publications

This guide will help you efficiently manage reviews and provide meaningful feedback.

## Getting Started

### Accessing Database Activities

1. Log in to Moodle
2. Navigate to the fellowship course
3. You'll see three database activities:
   - Case and Surgical Logbook
   - Credentialing Sheet
   - Research and Publications

### Your Permissions

As a mentor (Teacher role), you can:
- View all trainee submissions
- Approve or reject entries
- Provide feedback and comments
- Edit entries if needed
- Export data for reporting

## Case Logbook Review

### Review Workflow

1. **Access Pending Entries**
   - Click on "Case and Surgical Logbook"
   - Click "View list"
   - Filter by "Pending Review" status
   - Or use "Approval" tab to see all pending

2. **Review Each Entry**
   - Click on entry to view details
   - Check completeness and accuracy
   - Evaluate learning points
   - Assess surgical role appropriateness

3. **Provide Feedback**
   - Scroll to "Mentor Feedback" field
   - Click "Edit"
   - Enter your comments
   - Be specific and constructive

4. **Approve or Request Revision**
   - Change "Approval Status" to:
     - **Approved:** Entry is satisfactory
     - **Needs Revision:** Requires changes
     - **Rejected:** Significant issues
   - Click "Save"

### What to Look For

#### Completeness
- ✅ All required fields filled
- ✅ Adequate procedure details
- ✅ Clear outcomes documented
- ✅ Complications honestly reported
- ✅ Meaningful learning points

#### Accuracy
- ✅ Procedure details match surgical role
- ✅ Outcomes are realistic
- ✅ Complications appropriately managed
- ✅ Learning points show reflection

#### Red Flags
- ❌ Incomplete information
- ❌ Inconsistent details
- ❌ Unrealistic outcomes
- ❌ Missing complications in complex cases
- ❌ Generic learning points
- ❌ Patient identifying information

### Feedback Best Practices

**Good Feedback Examples:**

✅ "Excellent documentation. Your management of the posterior capsule rent was appropriate. Consider discussing the use of ophthalmic viscosurgical devices in your next similar case."

✅ "Good case. Please elaborate on your capsulorhexis technique - what size did you aim for and why?"

✅ "Well documented. Your learning point about patient positioning is important. How will you apply this in future cases?"

**Feedback to Avoid:**

❌ "OK" (too brief, not helpful)
❌ "Approved" (no learning value)
❌ "Good" (not specific enough)

### Batch Review Tips

For efficient review of multiple entries:

1. **Set Aside Dedicated Time**
   - Review 2-3 times per week
   - Allocate 5-10 minutes per entry
   - Don't let entries pile up

2. **Use Filters**
   - Filter by trainee
   - Filter by subspecialty
   - Filter by date range
   - Filter by status

3. **Prioritize**
   - Review oldest entries first
   - Focus on complex cases
   - Flag entries needing discussion

## Credentialing Sheet Verification

### Monthly Review Process

1. **Access Submissions**
   - Click on "Credentialing Sheet"
   - View entries for current month
   - Check submission dates

2. **Verify Procedure Counts**
   - Cross-reference with case logbook
   - Check for reasonable numbers
   - Verify competency claims
   - Look for patterns or gaps

3. **Review Competencies**
   - Assess competencies achieved
   - Verify skills demonstrated
   - Check alignment with training stage
   - Confirm progression

4. **Approve or Request Correction**
   - Add mentor comments
   - Change approval status
   - Specify any corrections needed
   - Set approval date

### Verification Checklist

- [ ] Procedure counts match case logbook entries
- [ ] Numbers are reasonable for training level
- [ ] All procedure categories addressed
- [ ] Competencies achieved are documented
- [ ] Skills demonstrated are appropriate
- [ ] No obvious errors or omissions

### Common Issues

**Discrepancies:**
- Counts don't match logbook
- Unrealistic numbers
- Missing categories

**Action:** Request correction with specific details

**Incomplete Submissions:**
- Missing competency information
- Vague skill descriptions
- No procedure counts

**Action:** Return for completion

**Late Submissions:**
- Submitted after deadline
- Multiple late submissions

**Action:** Approve but document pattern, discuss with trainee

**Daily (5-10 minutes):**
- Check for new submissions
- Quick review of urgent items
- Respond to trainee questions

- Review case logbook entries
- Provide detailed feedback
- Approve credentialing sheets
- Review research updates

**Monthly (1-2 hours):**
- Comprehensive review of all trainees

   - Create feedback snippets
   - Customize for each trainee

2. **Batch Similar Reviews**
   - Review all cataract cases together
   - Review all credentialing sheets together

3. **Set Expectations**
   - Communicate review timeline
   - Set office hours for questions
   - Establish submission standards

4. **Delegate When Appropriate**
   - Co-mentors can share review load
   - Senior residents can provide initial feedback
   - You provide final approval

## Providing Effective Feedback

### Feedback Framework: STAR

**S - Specific:** Point to exact details
**T - Timely:** Provide feedback promptly
**A - Actionable:** Give clear next steps
**R - Respectful:** Maintain professional tone

### Examples by Scenario

**Excellent Performance:**
"Your documentation of this complex vitrectomy case is exemplary. The detailed description of your approach to the retinal detachment repair and your reflection on the learning points demonstrate excellent clinical reasoning. Keep up this standard."

**Good with Room for Improvement:**
"Good case documentation. Your surgical technique description is clear. To enhance your learning, consider adding more detail about your decision-making process when you encountered the posterior capsule opacity. What factors influenced your choice of approach?"

**Needs Significant Improvement:**
"This entry needs more detail. Please expand on: 1) Your specific role in the procedure, 2) The complications you mentioned - how were they managed?, 3) Your learning points - what will you do differently next time? Please revise and resubmit."

**Concerning Pattern:**
"I've noticed your last three case entries have minimal learning points. Reflection is crucial for your development. Let's schedule a meeting to discuss how to get more from your clinical experiences."

   - Competency achievement rate

2. **Quality Indicators**
   - Documentation quality
   - Complication rates
   - Learning point depth
   - Response to feedback

3. **Professional Development**
   - Research engagement
   - Publication progress
   - Skill acquisition
   - Competency milestones

### Red Flags

Watch for these warning signs:

- ⚠️ Declining submission frequency
- ⚠️ Consistently incomplete entries
- ⚠️ No progression in surgical roles
- ⚠️ Avoiding certain subspecialties
- ⚠️ Not responding to feedback
- ⚠️ Late submissions becoming pattern
- ⚠️ Unrealistic or inflated counts

**Action:** Schedule one-on-one meeting to discuss

## Common Scenarios

4. Request correction

3. Identify barriers

5. Provide additional support
6. Monitor progress

**Solution:** Check notification preferences, verify email settings

**Solution:** Use Export tab, select format, contact admin if issues
