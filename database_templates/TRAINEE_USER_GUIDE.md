# Trainee User Guide - Database Activities

## Welcome!

This guide will help you use the three database activities for tracking your fellowship training:

1. **Case Logbook** - Record patient cases and surgical procedures
2. **Credentialing Sheet** - Submit monthly procedure counts
3. **Research Publications** - Track your research and publications

## Case Logbook

### Purpose
Document every patient case and surgical procedure you handle for mentor review and competency tracking.

### How to Add a Case Entry

1. **Access the Logbook**
   - Go to your course
   - Click on "Case and Surgical Logbook"
   - Click "Add entry"

2. **Fill Required Information**
   - **Case Date:** Date you handled the case
   - **Subspecialty:** Select from dropdown (Cataract, Retina, Cornea, etc.)
   - **Procedure Type:** Name of the procedure (e.g., "Phacoemulsification")
   - **Procedure Details:** Describe what you did in detail
   - **Diagnosis:** Patient's primary diagnosis
   - **Surgical Role:** Your role (Primary Surgeon, Assistant, Observer, Supervised Practice)
   - **Outcomes:** What happened - successful? complications?
   - **Complications:** Any issues (write "None" if no complications)
   - **Learning Points:** What did you learn from this case?

3. **Submit for Review**
   - Click "Save"
   - Your entry will show "Pending Review" status
   - Your mentor will review and provide feedback

### Tips for Case Logbook

✅ **Do:**
- Submit cases promptly (same day or next day)
- Be detailed in procedure descriptions
- Be honest about complications
- Reflect on learning points
- Check for mentor feedback regularly

❌ **Don't:**
- Include patient identifying information (names, IDs)
- Submit incomplete entries
- Wait until end of month to submit all cases
- Ignore mentor feedback

### Monthly Submission Requirement
- Submit all cases by the last day of each month
- Ensure all entries are approved before next month
- Follow up on entries needing revision

## Credentialing Sheet

### Purpose
Submit monthly summary of all procedures performed and competencies achieved.

### How to Submit Monthly Credentialing

1. **Access Credentialing Sheet**
   - Go to your course
   - Click on "Credentialing Sheet"
   - Click "Add entry"

2. **Fill Procedure Counts**
   - **Month & Year:** Select current month and year
   - **Submission Date:** Today's date
   - **Procedure Counts:** Enter accurate counts for each procedure type
     - Cataract procedures (Phaco, SICS, IOL)
     - Retina procedures (Vitrectomy, Laser, Injections)
     - Cornea procedures (Keratoplasty, Pterygium, Cross-linking)
     - Glaucoma procedures (Trabeculectomy, Laser, Tube Shunt)
     - Oculoplasty procedures (Lid Surgery, DCR, Orbital)
     - Pediatric procedures (Strabismus, Pediatric Cataract)
   - **Competencies Achieved:** List competencies you completed this month
   - **Skills Demonstrated:** Describe skills you demonstrated

3. **Submit for Verification**
   - Double-check all counts are accurate
   - Click "Save"
   - Wait for mentor verification

### Tips for Credentialing Sheet

✅ **Do:**
- Keep daily tally of procedures
- Submit by deadline (last day of month)
- Verify counts against your case logbook
- Be accurate - this is used for accreditation
- Save draft if not complete

❌ **Don't:**
- Inflate numbers
- Submit late
- Forget to include all procedure types
- Submit without reviewing for accuracy

### Tracking Your Progress
- Review previous months to see progression
- Compare procedure counts over time
- Identify areas needing more exposure
- Discuss gaps with your mentor

## Research and Publications

### Purpose
Track research projects, publications, and academic contributions throughout your training.

### How to Add Research Entry

1. **Access Research Database**
   - Go to your course
   - Click on "Research and Publications"
   - Click "Add entry"

2. **For Research Proposals**
   - **Title:** Your research project title
   - **Research Type:** Select "Research Proposal"
   - **Status:** Select "Planning"
   - **Subspecialty:** Select relevant area
   - **Abstract:** Describe your proposed research
   - **Keywords:** Add relevant keywords
   - **Methodology:** Describe your research approach
   - **Primary Mentor:** Your research supervisor's name
   - Click "Save" and wait for mentor review

3. **For Publications**
   - **Title:** Publication title
   - **Research Type:** Select type (Journal Article, Conference Presentation, etc.)
   - **Status:** Current status (Submitted, Accepted, Published, etc.)
   - **Authors:** Complete author list in order
   - **Publication Year:** Year published or expected
   - **Journal/Conference:** Name of journal or conference
   - **Volume/Issue, Pages:** Publication details
   - **DOI/Link:** Add link to publication
   - **Abstract:** Publication abstract
   - **Keywords:** Relevant keywords
   - Click "Save"

### Tips for Research Tracking

✅ **Do:**
- Submit proposals before starting research
- Update status regularly
- Include complete publication metadata
- Link to online publications
- Maintain accurate author lists
- Respond to mentor feedback promptly

❌ **Don't:**
- Start research without mentor approval
- Forget to update status
- Submit incomplete publication information
- Claim authorship incorrectly

### Research Workflow

1. **Proposal Stage**
   - Submit research proposal
   - Wait for mentor review
   - Revise based on feedback
   - Get approval to proceed

2. **Execution Stage**
   - Update status to "Data Collection" or "Analysis"
   - Keep mentor informed of progress
   - Submit interim updates if needed

3. **Publication Stage**
   - Update status to "Writing" then "Submitted"
   - Add publication details when accepted
   - Update to "Published" with final details
   - Share link to published work

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I edit after submission?
**A:** Yes, until your mentor approves it. After approval, editing may be restricted.

### Q: What if I make a mistake?
**A:** Edit the entry before approval. After approval, contact your mentor or administrator.

### Q: How long does approval take?
**A:** Usually within 3-5 days. Follow up if longer.

### Q: Can I see other trainees' entries?
**A:** No, entries are private between you and your mentors.

### Q: What if I miss the monthly deadline?
**A:** Submit as soon as possible and inform your mentor. Repeated late submissions may affect your evaluation.

### Q: Can I export my data?
**A:** Yes, you can export your entries for your personal records. Ask your administrator for help.

### Q: What if I have technical issues?
**A:** Contact your course administrator or IT support immediately.

## Appendix: Field Descriptions

### Case Logbook Fields Explained

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| Case Date | Date of procedure | 15-Jan-2026 |
| Subspecialty | Category | Cataract |
| Procedure Type | Procedure name | Phacoemulsification with IOL |
| Procedure Details | What you did | "Performed phaco through 2.8mm clear corneal incision..." |
| Patient Age | Age in years | 65 |
| Diagnosis | Primary diagnosis | Senile cataract, right eye |
| Surgical Role | Your role | Primary Surgeon |
| Outcomes | Results | "Successful. BCVA 6/9 on day 1" |
| Complications | Any issues | "None" or "Posterior capsule rent, managed with..." |
| Learning Points | What you learned | "Improved my capsulorhexis technique" |

### Credentialing Sheet Procedure Counts

Count each procedure where you had a significant role (primary surgeon, assistant, or supervised practice). Do not count cases where you were only observing.

### Research Status Definitions

- **Planning:** Developing proposal
- **Data Collection:** Gathering data
- **Analysis:** Analyzing results
- **Writing:** Writing manuscript
- **Submitted:** Submitted to journal
- **Under Review:** In peer review
- **Accepted:** Accepted for publication
- **Published:** Published and available
- **Presented:** Presented at conference
