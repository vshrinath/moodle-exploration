# Video Tutorial Scripts - Database Activities

## Overview

These scripts can be used to create video tutorials for trainees, mentors, and administrators. Each script is designed for a 5-10 minute video.

---

## Tutorial 1: Trainee - Submitting Your First Case Logbook Entry

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Audience:** New trainees
**Prerequisites:** Moodle account, enrolled in course

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome! In this tutorial, you'll learn how to submit your first case logbook entry. The case logbook is where you document every surgical case you handle during your fellowship. Let's get started!"

**[0:30-1:30] Accessing the Logbook**
"First, log in to Moodle and navigate to your fellowship course. You'll see an activity called 'Case and Surgical Logbook.' Click on it. This opens the logbook where all your cases will be stored. To add a new case, click the 'Add entry' button."

**[1:30-3:00] Filling Basic Information**
"Now let's fill in the case details. Start with the case date - this is the date you performed or assisted with the procedure. Next, select the subspecialty from the dropdown menu. We have seven subspecialties: Cataract, Retina, Cornea, Glaucoma, Oculoplasty, Pediatric, and Neuro-Ophthalmology. For this example, I'll select Cataract."

**[3:00-4:30] Procedure Details**
"In the Procedure Type field, enter the name of the procedure - for example, 'Phacoemulsification with IOL implantation.' In Procedure Details, describe what you did in detail. Be specific about your technique, any challenges you faced, and how you addressed them. Add the patient's age and primary diagnosis."

**[4:30-5:30] Surgical Role and Outcomes**
"Select your surgical role from the dropdown. Were you the primary surgeon, assistant surgeon, observer, or doing supervised practice? Be honest - this helps track your progression. In the Outcomes field, describe the results. Was the surgery successful? What was the visual outcome? Be factual and complete."

**[5:30-6:30] Complications and Learning**
"In the Complications field, document any complications honestly. If there were none, write 'None.' If there were complications, describe them and how they were managed. This is crucial for learning. In Learning Points, reflect on what you learned. What would you do differently? What technique did you improve? This reflection is valuable for your development."

**[6:30-7:30] Submission and Approval**
"The Approval Status will default to 'Pending Review.' Leave the Mentor Feedback field empty - your mentor will fill this in. Double-check all your entries, then click 'Save.' Your entry is now submitted for mentor review. You'll receive an email notification when your mentor reviews it."

**[7:30-8:00] Closing**
"That's it! You've submitted your first case logbook entry. Remember to submit cases regularly, not all at once at the end of the month. Check back for mentor feedback and respond to any revision requests. Happy learning!"

## Tutorial 2: Trainee - Submitting Monthly Credentialing Sheet

**Duration:** 7 minutes
**Audience:** Trainees
**Prerequisites:** Completed at least one month of training

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome! This tutorial will show you how to submit your monthly credentialing sheet. This is a summary of all procedures you performed during the month and is used for accreditation purposes. Accuracy is critical!"

**[0:30-1:30] Preparation**
"Before you start, gather your data. Review your case logbook entries for the month and tally your procedures by type. It's best to keep a running count throughout the month rather than counting at the end. Open the Credentialing Sheet activity and click 'Add entry.'"

**[1:30-2:30] Month and Date**
"Select the month and year you're reporting. Enter today's date as the submission date. Make sure you're submitting for the correct month - you can only submit one credentialing sheet per month."

**[2:30-4:30] Procedure Counts**
"Now enter your procedure counts. Go through each category carefully:
- Cataract: Phaco, SICS/ECCE, IOL implantations
- Retina: Vitrectomy, laser, injections
- Cornea: Keratoplasty, pterygium, cross-linking
- Glaucoma: Trabeculectomy, laser, tube shunts
- Oculoplasty: Lid surgery, DCR, orbital procedures
- Pediatric: Strabismus, pediatric cataract

Enter '0' for categories where you had no procedures. Don't leave any fields blank."

**[4:30-5:30] Competencies and Skills**
"In the Competencies Achieved field, list the specific competencies you completed this month. Be specific - for example, 'Completed 10 supervised phacoemulsification procedures' or 'Achieved competency in basic vitrectomy setup.' In Skills Demonstrated, describe the skills you practiced or mastered."

**[5:30-6:30] Review and Submit**
"Before submitting, double-check your numbers against your case logbook. Make sure all counts are accurate. The approval status will be set to 'Pending Verification.' Click 'Save' to submit. Your mentor will verify your counts and provide feedback."

**[6:30-7:00] Closing**
"Remember, this data is used for accreditation and certification. Be accurate and honest. Submit by the last day of each month. If you have questions about what to count, ask your mentor. Good luck!"

## Tutorial 3: Mentor - Reviewing and Approving Case Logbook Entries

**Duration:** 10 minutes
**Audience:** Mentors
**Prerequisites:** Teacher role in course

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome, mentors! This tutorial will show you how to efficiently review and approve trainee case logbook entries. Your feedback is crucial for trainee development, so let's make it count!"

**[0:30-1:30] Accessing Pending Entries**
"Log in to Moodle and navigate to the fellowship course. Click on 'Case and Surgical Logbook.' You'll see a list of entries. To see only entries awaiting your review, click on the 'Approval' tab or use the filter to show 'Pending Review' entries. This helps you focus on what needs attention."

**[1:30-3:00] Reviewing an Entry**
"Click on an entry to open it. Review all the fields carefully. Check that the procedure details are complete and accurate. Verify that the surgical role matches the complexity of the procedure. Review the outcomes - are they realistic? Check the complications field - is it honest and complete? Read the learning points - do they show genuine reflection?"

**[3:00-4:30] What to Look For**
"Look for completeness, accuracy, and reflection. Red flags include: incomplete information, unrealistic outcomes, missing complications in complex cases, generic learning points, or patient identifying information. If you see any of these, the entry needs revision."

**[4:30-6:30] Providing Feedback**
"Click 'Edit' on the entry. Scroll to the Mentor Feedback field. This is where you add your comments. Be specific and constructive. Instead of just 'Good,' say 'Good documentation. Your management of the capsular tear was appropriate. Consider discussing the use of capsular tension rings in your next similar case.' Give actionable advice that helps them improve."

**[6:30-8:00] Approving or Requesting Revision**
"Change the Approval Status based on your review:
- Select 'Approved' if the entry is satisfactory
- Select 'Needs Revision' if changes are required - be specific about what needs to change
- Select 'Rejected' only for serious issues

Click 'Save' to submit your review. The trainee will receive an email notification with your feedback."

**[8:00-9:00] Batch Review Tips**
"For efficiency, set aside dedicated time 2-3 times per week. Use filters to organize entries by trainee, subspecialty, or date. Review oldest entries first. Allocate 5-10 minutes per entry. Don't let entries pile up - timely feedback is more valuable."

**[9:00-10:00] Closing**
"Remember, your feedback shapes their learning. Be specific, timely, and constructive. Track patterns across entries to identify areas where trainees need more support. Thank you for your dedication to training!"

## Tutorial 4: Administrator - Importing Database Templates

**Duration:** 6 minutes
**Audience:** Administrators, course creators
**Prerequisites:** Manager or course creator role

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome, administrators! This tutorial will show you how to import the pre-configured database templates for the ophthalmology fellowship. We'll import the Case Logbook template, but the process is the same for all three templates."

**[0:30-1:30] Creating the Database Activity**
"Navigate to your fellowship course and turn editing on. Click 'Add an activity or resource' in the section where you want the database. Select 'Database' from the list and click 'Add.' Enter a name like 'Case and Surgical Logbook' and optionally add a description. Click 'Save and display.'"

**[1:30-3:00] Importing the Template**
"You'll see several tabs at the top. Click on the 'Presets' tab. This is where we import our template. Click 'Import' then choose 'Upload preset.' Click 'Choose a file' and select the case_logbook_template.xml file from your computer. Click 'Upload this file' then 'Import.' You'll see a preview of what will be imported. Click 'Continue' to confirm."

**[3:00-4:00] Configuring Settings**
"The template is now imported! Click on the 'Settings' icon to configure important options. Under 'Approval,' enable 'Require approval' so mentors must approve entries. Under 'Availability,' you can set submission windows if needed. Under 'Common module settings,' configure completion tracking if desired."

**[4:00-5:00] Setting Permissions**
"Go to the course participants page. Ensure mentors are enrolled with the Teacher role. Verify they have the 'mod/data:approve' capability. Trainees should have the Student role with 'mod/data:writeentry' capability. Test the workflow by creating a test entry and approving it."

**[5:00-6:00] Closing**
"That's it! Repeat this process for the Credentialing Sheet and Research Publications templates. Refer to the README file for detailed configuration options and troubleshooting. The templates are ready to use immediately after import!"

## Tutorial 5: Trainee - Adding Research Projects and Publications

**Duration:** 9 minutes
**Audience:** Trainees
**Prerequisites:** Enrolled in course

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome! This tutorial will show you how to use the Research and Publications database to track your academic work throughout your fellowship. We'll cover both research proposals and publications."

**[0:30-2:00] Submitting a Research Proposal**
"Let's start with a research proposal. Open the Research and Publications activity and click 'Add entry.' Enter your research title in the Title field. For Research Type, select 'Research Proposal.' Set Status to 'Planning.' Choose the relevant subspecialty. In the Abstract field, describe your proposed research - what question are you trying to answer? Why is it important?"

**[2:00-3:30] Proposal Details**
"Add keywords that describe your research - these help others find your work later. In the Methodology field, describe how you'll conduct the research. What's your study design? What data will you collect? How will you analyze it? You can leave Key Findings empty for now - you'll fill this in later. Enter your primary mentor's name. The Review Status will default to 'Pending Review.' Click 'Save' to submit your proposal."

**[3:30-5:00] Updating Research Status**
"Once your mentor approves your proposal, you'll update the status as you progress. Click 'Edit' on your entry. Change Status to 'Data Collection' when you start collecting data, then 'Analysis' when analyzing, then 'Writing' when writing your manuscript. Add key findings as they emerge. Keep your mentor informed by updating regularly."

**[5:00-7:00] Adding a Publication**
"When you publish, create a new entry or update your existing one. Change Research Type to the appropriate publication type - Journal Article, Conference Presentation, etc. Update Status to 'Published' or 'Presented.' Fill in all publication details: complete author list in order, publication year, journal or conference name, volume and issue, page numbers, and most importantly, the DOI or link to the publication. This makes your work accessible to others."

**[7:00-8:00] Publication Metadata**
"Complete the abstract, keywords, methodology, and key findings. If it's a journal article, add the impact factor if available. Make sure all information is accurate - this will be part of your academic portfolio. Change Review Status to 'Approved - Published' after mentor review."

**[8:00-9:00] Closing**
"Your research database serves as your academic portfolio. Keep it updated throughout your fellowship. It's useful for CV preparation, job applications, and tracking your academic growth. Submit proposals before starting research, update status regularly, and add all publications promptly. Good luck with your research!"

## Tutorial 6: Mentor - Reviewing Research Proposals

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Audience:** Mentors
**Prerequisites:** Teacher role in course

### Script

**[0:00-0:30] Introduction**
"Welcome, mentors! This tutorial focuses on reviewing research proposals in the Research and Publications database. Your guidance at the proposal stage is crucial for research success."

**[0:30-1:30] Accessing Research Proposals**
"Open the Research and Publications activity. Filter entries by Research Type 'Research Proposal' and Review Status 'Pending Review.' This shows you all proposals awaiting your review. Click on a proposal to open it."

**[1:30-3:30] Evaluating the Proposal**
"Review the proposal systematically. First, assess the research question - is it clear, relevant, and feasible? Check the abstract - does it explain the rationale and significance? Review the methodology - is it appropriate for the research question? Is it feasible with available resources? Consider ethical implications - will it need ethics approval? Think about timeline - can it be completed within the fellowship period?"

**[3:30-5:00] Providing Feedback**
"Click 'Edit' on the entry. In the Mentor Feedback field, provide detailed, constructive feedback. Comment on strengths first, then areas for improvement. Be specific: 'Your research question is well-defined. Consider expanding your methodology section to include your sampling strategy and sample size calculation. Have you considered potential confounding variables?' Suggest resources, references, or collaborators that might help."

**[5:00-6:30] Setting Review Status**
"Change the Review Status based on your assessment:
- 'Approved to Proceed' if the proposal is ready - the trainee can start research
- 'Needs Revision' if changes are required - be specific about what needs to change
- 'Rejected' if the proposal is not feasible - explain why and suggest alternatives

Set a Review Date to today's date. Click 'Save.'"

**[6:30-7:30] Ongoing Monitoring**
"After approval, monitor progress. When trainees update their status, review the updates. Provide guidance when they face challenges. Celebrate milestones. When they're ready to submit for publication, review the manuscript if appropriate. Update Review Status to 'Approved for Submission' when ready."

**[7:30-8:00] Closing**
"Your mentorship in research is invaluable. Provide timely, detailed feedback. Encourage rigorous methodology. Support them through challenges. Help them develop as researchers. Thank you for your guidance!"

## Production Notes

### General Guidelines for All Videos

**Visual Elements:**
- Screen recording of actual Moodle interface
- Highlight cursor movements
- Zoom in on important fields
- Use callout boxes for key points
- Show before/after states

**Audio:**
- Clear, professional narration
- Moderate pace - not too fast
- Pause at transitions
- Emphasize key terms
- Friendly, encouraging tone

**Editing:**
- Add chapter markers
- Include on-screen text for key points
- Use transitions between sections
- Add intro/outro graphics
- Include contact information for support

**Accessibility:**
- Provide closed captions
- Include transcript
- Use high contrast for visibility
- Ensure text is readable
- Provide audio descriptions if needed

### Distribution

**Where to Host:**
- Moodle course (embedded)
- YouTube (unlisted or public)
- Institutional video platform
- Download option for offline viewing

**Organization:**
- Create playlist for each user type
- Tag videos appropriately
- Include in course orientation
- Link from user guides
- Update as system changes

### Maintenance

**When to Update:**
- Moodle interface changes
- Template modifications
- Workflow changes
- User feedback indicates confusion
- Annual review
