# SCEH Rules Engine - User Guide

## For Learners

### Understanding Competency Locking

Some competencies in your learning path may be locked based on your attendance. This ensures you have sufficient foundational knowledge before progressing to advanced topics.

#### Why is a Competency Locked?

You'll see a message like:
> "This competency is locked due to insufficient attendance (65% of 75% required)"

This means:
- You need to attend **75%** of sessions
- Your current attendance is **65%**
- You need to attend **10% more** sessions to unlock this competency

#### How to Unlock a Competency

1. **Attend More Sessions**: Improve your attendance in the related course
2. **Check Progress**: Your attendance percentage updates automatically after each session
3. **Access Unlocked**: Once you meet the threshold, the competency becomes available immediately

#### Automatic Competency Awards

Some competencies are awarded automatically when you complete specific activities:

- **Morning Class Roster**: Completing morning classes may award attendance competencies
- **Night Duty**: Completing night duty shifts awards emergency response competencies
- **Training OT**: Operating theatre rotations award surgical exposure competencies
- **Satellite Visits**: Community visits award outreach competencies
- **Postings**: Department postings award specialty-specific competencies

You don't need to do anything special - the system tracks your roster completions and awards evidence automatically.

### Viewing Your Progress

1. Navigate to your **Learning Plan**
2. View competencies with their status:
   - ✓ **Completed**: You've achieved proficiency
   - 🔒 **Locked**: Attendance requirement not met
   - ⏳ **In Progress**: Available but not yet completed
3. Click on any competency to see:
   - Evidence collected
   - Attendance requirements (if applicable)
   - Next steps to achieve proficiency

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often is my attendance updated?**
A: Attendance is calculated in real-time after each session is marked by your trainer.

**Q: Can I appeal a locked competency?**
A: Contact your program coordinator if you believe there's an error in your attendance calculation.

**Q: Do excused absences count against me?**
A: This depends on your program's attendance policy. Check with your coordinator.

**Q: How do I know which rosters award competencies?**
A: Your program guide lists which roster types are linked to specific competencies.

## For Trainers

### Attendance and Competency Access

When you mark attendance, the system automatically:
1. Calculates each learner's attendance percentage
2. Evaluates competency access rules
3. Blocks or unblocks competencies based on thresholds
4. Logs all actions for audit purposes

#### Best Practices

- **Mark Attendance Promptly**: Delays affect learner access to competencies
- **Use Correct Status**: Ensure Present/Absent/Excused are marked accurately
- **Communicate Thresholds**: Inform learners of attendance requirements upfront
- **Monitor Progress**: Review learner dashboards to identify those at risk

### Roster Completions

When you mark roster completions in the Scheduler:
1. System checks for competency rules linked to that roster type
2. Automatically creates competency evidence for the learner
3. Records the evidence with description
4. Logs the action in audit trail

#### Verifying Automatic Awards

1. Navigate to learner's **Competency Profile**
2. View **Evidence** tab
3. Look for entries marked "Automatically awarded for completing [roster type] roster"
4. Verify evidence was created with correct timestamp

### Troubleshooting

**Issue**: Learner says competency should be unlocked but still shows locked

**Steps**:
1. Verify attendance has been marked for all sessions
2. Check learner's actual attendance percentage
3. Compare to competency threshold requirement
4. Contact administrator if calculation appears incorrect

**Issue**: Roster completion didn't award competency

**Steps**:
1. Verify roster type matches rule configuration
2. Check if learner already has proficiency for that competency
3. Review audit log for error messages
4. Contact administrator if issue persists

## For Program Coordinators

### Monitoring Rule Effectiveness

#### Attendance Impact Analysis

1. Review audit log monthly
2. Identify competencies with high block rates
3. Analyze if thresholds are appropriate
4. Adjust rules if needed (work with administrator)

#### Roster Automation Verification

1. Spot-check automatic competency awards
2. Verify evidence descriptions are clear
3. Ensure roster types map correctly to competencies
4. Review learner feedback on automation

### Communicating with Learners

#### At Program Start

Explain to learners:
- Which competencies have attendance requirements
- Specific attendance thresholds
- How roster completions award competencies
- Where to view their progress

#### During Program

- Send reminders about attendance thresholds
- Highlight learners at risk of competency locks
- Celebrate automatic competency achievements
- Address questions about rule logic

### Reporting

#### Monthly Reports

Generate reports showing:
- Number of competency blocks due to attendance
- Automatic competency awards by roster type
- Learners with multiple locked competencies
- Trends in attendance-related blocks

#### Accreditation Reports

Use audit log to demonstrate:
- Attendance tracking rigor
- Competency progression controls
- Evidence of automated assessment
- Compliance with training standards
